Market Reaction

A genuinely two-sided after-hours tape

  • Shares swung between roughly $337 and $350 in after-hours trading (prior close ~$342), before settling lower — reports at the time of writing showed GOOGL down approximately 1%–5% depending on the exact snapshot.
  • Initial reaction was positive on the cloud beat and RPO number, then faded as analysts and the earnings call turned to the capex guidance raise.
  • Some sell-side voices (e.g., Deepwater's Gene Munster) argued the stock should be higher given the scale of the cloud beat, calling the muted reaction a function of a slight search miss and capex optics rather than fundamentals.
  • Alphabet entered the print up roughly 4% over the trailing three months, outperforming Meta and Microsoft, roughly flat vs. Amazon, over the same window.

Forward Watch List

Five things that will drive the next leg

  1. Cloud margin trajectory — op. margin already stepped up to ~35% from ~14% a year ago; can it hold as capacity comes online?
  2. 2027 capex commentary — management flagged spend increasing "significantly" again next year; watch for a number at the next print.
  3. Search growth durability — 17% growth is strong but decelerated from Q1's 19%; AI Overviews monetization and Gemini 4 competitive response are the swing factors.
  4. RPO conversion — the $514B backlog needs to convert into recognized Cloud revenue on schedule to justify the capex.
  5. Free cash flow — FCF has been under pressure from the capex ramp; a stabilizing FCF print would be the clearest "de-risking" signal for the stock.

Positioning Note

Trade plan across the three reaction scenarios

Gap up / rally

If the market ultimately rewards the Cloud beat and RPO number, existing longs/LEAPS can be held; avoid chasing the first green candle — let the capex narrative settle for a session.

Gap down / fade

A capex-led selloff toward prior support is a better risk/reward entry point for a long than chasing strength, given the underlying operating trend is intact.

Flat / chop

Two-sided, low-conviction tape (as seen in the immediate after-hours action) argues for patience — wait for the next 2–3 sessions of institutional positioning to clarify direction before adding size.

Bottom line: the operating business (Cloud, margins, RPO) is stronger than the tape suggests; the headline EPS beat is not — separate the two before sizing any post-earnings trade.
